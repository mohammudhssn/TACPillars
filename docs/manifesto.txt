3. The Core Idea: Every Piece of Knowledge Has a Correct Size
This entire model comes down to one principle:
The size of the idea determines where it belongs.
Huge ideas become Pillars.
Large themes become Sub-Pillars.
Conceptual pathways become Branches.
Teachable units become Topic Domains.
Conceptual components become Subtopics.
Atomic ideas become Knowledge Elements.
Formats and assets come last, because content is not the structure.
Structure is the structure.
When people confuse the sizes of ideas, content collapses.
When people respect the size hierarchy, content becomes infinitely scalable.
________________


4. The Hierarchy (Explained the Only Way That Matters)
Forget jargon.
Think of this as physics.
A Pillar holds enough intellectual mass to anchor an entire domain. When TAC has ten thousand pieces of content, Pillars will still make sense.
A Sub-Pillar is a major division inside that domain. If a Pillar is the ecosystem, Sub-Pillars are the biomes.
A Branch is the way your mind organizes a major problem. It’s a pattern of reasoning, not a category.
A Topic Domain is the first level that becomes a full, standalone product. It is a single solvable question with many components.
A Subtopic breaks the question into its conceptual parts.
A Knowledge Element is the smallest unit of meaning. Break it further and it stops being instruction and becomes noise.
Formats and assets are containers. They matter, but they are not the architecture. They are the output the architecture enables.
If you get this, you get the system.

5. The Rules That Hold the System Together
These rules are non-negotiable.
Break them, and the entire structure loses integrity.
Rule 1: Scope Purity
Every layer only does the job assigned to its layer.
Nothing more.
Nothing less.
Rule 2: Exclusive Placement
Each idea appears exactly once in the entire system.
If it appears twice, the system becomes untrustworthy.
Rule 3: Atomic Precision
The smaller units must be truly small.
No multi-idea blobs.
No ambiguous categories pretending to be concepts.
When contributors understand these, everything they create clicks into place.
When AI understands these, it becomes a disciplined knowledge engine.
